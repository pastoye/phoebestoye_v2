Hi, I'm Phoebe. I translate scientific research into health impact.


I currently do Product @ Clinical Research IO, where I launched Caretalk, a community-driven platform connecting families and experts in Alzheimer's and Type 2 Diabetes.

Through my experience growing up in the Silicon Valley, then Western Colorado, I saw dichotomies in peoples' access to information and resources, which can prevent them from living healthy lives. This, plus my interest in science and data, led me to study Neurobiology at Harvard ('18), where I did research on the neural effects of malnutrition through Boston Children's Hospital, and cofounded Chirp, an online community that connects teen parents to local and national programs and resources.

I am also an artist, two-time Head of the Charles coxswain, and outdoor explorer.

Caretalk (via Clinical Research IO)

Product Manager

In my Product Manager role at CRIO, my team developed & launched the patient-facing subsidiary, tackling the issue that about 50% of clinical trials fail because they do not enroll enough participants. I researched the product, led a team of seven software engineers, UX designers, and marketers, did UX design, interfaced with the executive team & investors, and recruited and managed a Board of Medical Advisors for the product.

The product's alpha included a clinically-reviewed editorial pipeline, a personalized medication search and comparison, a clinical trial search, profiles with messaging and following capabilities, and much more.

Z-projection of Zebrafish brain expressing G-Camp

Taken on a Lightsheet microscope, courtesy of MCB @ Harvard

This image of a zebrafish brain was taken during a microscopy class in college. However, separately, I worked in a zebrafish lab, where I designed & built a microscope to image the brains of optogenetically-modified, larval zebrafish. This means that we could stimulate different types of neurons with light, then image which neurons were being activated and track the fish's behavior.
